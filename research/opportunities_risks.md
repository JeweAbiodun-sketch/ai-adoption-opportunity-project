## Opportunity Map

### Opportunity 1 — AI Demand Forecasting & Inventory Optimisation (INVEST — Start Here)
**Evidence strength:** 9/10 — multiple published case studies at mid-market scale

- Inventory cost reduction: 10–20%
- Overstock and stockout reduction: 30–50%
- Peak-to-trough revenue swing in dataset: 76% (Nov £51K peak, Feb £29K trough) — buyers are guessing every replenishment cycle
- Revenue concentrated in Clothing & Electronics: 69% — pilot forecasting here first
- Payback period: 12–18 months (not 3–6; model needs a full seasonal cycle)
- Entry-level vendors: Inventory Planner (~£99/mo), Relex Lite, Brightpearl AI
- **Why first:** Seasonal complexity and margin pressure make this the highest-ROI entry point. Works on existing ERP/POS data. No customer data platform required. Low hype risk (4/10).
- **Estimated annual ROI (base case, 60% attribution):** £405,000

### Opportunity 2 — Customer Personalisation & Recommendations (INVEST — Phase 2)
**Evidence strength:** 7/10 — conditional on data readiness

- Revenue uplift: 15–25% on targeted channels
- Repeat purchase rate improvement: 10–15%
- 84% of customers split across 4 equal age segments — clear segmentation target
- **Prerequisite:** Unified customer view with 12+ months history; deploy after demand forecasting is stable
- **Estimated annual ROI:** £300,000

### Opportunity 3 — AI Customer Service & Returns Automation (PILOT — not INVEST)
**Evidence strength:** 8/10 — but hype 8/10 creates risk

- Realistic cost reduction: 30–50% on tier-1 queries (not the vendor-claimed 80%)
- Returns processing time reduction: 40–60%
- Best for: High-returns categories (electronics fits)
- **Caution:** Chatbot '80% cost savings' claims are widely oversold. Negotiate service-level terms explicitly. Pilot with a limited query category before any broad deployment.
- **Estimated annual ROI:** £240,000

### Opportunity 4 — Dynamic Pricing & Markdown Optimisation (PILOT)
**Evidence strength:** 6/10 — growing

- Margin improvement: 3–8%
- Clearance sell-through improvement: 20–40%
- Risk: Customer perception risk if pricing changes are visible and unexplained
- **Estimated annual ROI:** £180,000

### Opportunity 5 — Content AI (PILOT)
**Evidence strength:** 4/10

- Content production cost reduction: 40–60%
- Limited direct revenue impact at this scale
- **Estimated annual ROI:** £85,000

## Risk Map — Rated by Likelihood & Impact

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Data gaps block Phase 1 | HIGH | HIGH | Conditional 3–6 month remediation phase (£30K–£60K); run audit before any spend |
| Buyer team resists AI | HIGH | HIGH | Shadow mode first — AI advises, buyer decides; named merchandising champion accountable for adoption |
| Adoption <70% at Week 16 | MED | HIGH | Pause and fix — do not proceed to full rollout below threshold |
| Vendor oversells accuracy | MED | MED | Negotiate MAPE performance clause into contract before signing |
| ROI decision made too early | MED | HIGH | Phase 3 (Wks 17–20) is operational review only; ROI gate = Month 12–18 |
| Infrastructure sticker shock at scale | HIGH | MED | Present hybrid TCO (£393K/3yr) alongside SaaS pilot cost from day one |
| IT-led ownership (no business champion) | HIGH | HIGH | Merchandising champion must be a business lead, not IT. Named before Phase 2 begins. |

*Three risks are rated HIGH × HIGH — data quality, buyer resistance, and IT-led ownership. Each has a named mitigation. All three must be addressed before Phase 2 begins.*
