What can still derail this

Three risks that kill AI projects at your stage

Data quality will block deployment
Missing historical inventory, incomplete POS records, no unified customer view. 3–6 months of data remediation needed before training can begin. Deploying on bad data produces unreliable forecasts that destroy trust.
IT-led AI with no business ownership
Buyers and merchandisers won't adopt tools they didn't help design. Without a named business champion and cross-functional governance, tools will be built and ignored.
Workforce skills gap — 30–50% underutilisation risk
Most staff rely on manual processes. Without role-based training and 20–30 hours of protected learning time per person, even well-designed tools will underperform in the first 6 months.
The recommended path

A four-phase rollout

0
Data & Process Audit · Weeks 1–4 · £5k–£8k
Audit sales, inventory, and customer data quality. Map buying/merchandising processes. Interview 8–10 staff. If data gaps need 9+ months to fix — pause and fix foundations first.
1
Vendor Evaluation · Weeks 5–8
Free trials of 2–3 SaaS forecasting tools on one category, one store. Buyers involved from day one.
2
Pilot Deployment · Weeks 9–24
Shadow mode testing alongside current process. Role-based training, visible champions, daily tool integration. Target: >70% active usage by week 24.
3
ROI Review & Scale Decision · Month 12–18
Compare actual inventory cost, stockout rate, and markdown rate against baseline. Scale to all stores or stop. Do not evaluate earlier — one season is not enough data.

How we measure success

Four KPIs. One decision rule.

Inventory days-on-hand
−15%
Less capital tied up in slow-moving stock
Stockout rate
−30%
Fewer lost sales on high-demand SKUs
Markdown rate
−20%
Less end-of-life discounting on electronics
Forecast accuracy (MAPE)
<15%
Mean absolute percentage error threshold

Decision rule: Measure at Month 12 and Month 18 — not earlier. If two of four KPIs are not met at Month 18, stop and reassess. Do not extend a failing pilot.
